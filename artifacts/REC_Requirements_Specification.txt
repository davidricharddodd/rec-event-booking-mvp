1. Executive Summary

The Recruitment & Employment Confederation (REC) requires a replacement for its existing Cvent-based event management platform. This document captures the full requirements for a custom-built solution that will serve as the primary system for managing, publishing, and processing registrations for REC events — including free and paid seminars, CPD workshops, Legal Helpline Q&As, and the Annual Conference.

A vendor discovery call confirmed the proposed approach: a React/Next.js + Node.js/Python + PostgreSQL web platform, paired with a React Native mobile app, hosted on Azure, and integrated with REC's Dynamics 365 CRM and CS5 website. The vendor's fixed-price proposal is £75,000 (one-off build) with a £3,000–£4,000/year maintenance retainer, on a 21-week delivery timeline.

1.1 Objectives
	•	Replace Cvent with a fully-owned, cost-efficient event platform that integrates natively with Dynamics 365.
	•	Support all current REC event types with no loss of capability.
	•	Provide a seamless registration and payment experience for members and non-members alike.
	•	Enable self-service event management for the REC events team with no vendor dependency for day-to-day operations.
	•	Deliver a companion mobile app for staff check-in and, in Phase 2, attendee engagement.
	•	Achieve GDPR and PCI-DSS compliance from day one.

1.2 Scope Summary
In Scope
Event creation & management, registration engine, payment (Stripe), email automation, CRM integration (Dynamics 365), CS5 website widget, staff mobile app, reporting & dashboards, RBAC, GDPR controls, Cvent data migration, Video Transcription App.
Out of Scope
Dynamics 365 CRM customisation (beyond agreed API integration points), CS5 CMS development, Eventbrite-style public marketplace listing, physical hardware for badge printing (vendor-supplied peripheral).
Phase 1
Core platform, staff app, CRM integration, CS5 widget, Stripe payments, email workflows, reporting.
Phase 2
Attendee-facing mobile app (combined staff+attendee), QR badge scanning at Annual Conference, AI event creation assistant.
Excluded Systems
HR Henry Copilot, Audit Online, Marketing Automation Platform (separate workstreams).

2.2 User Personas
Persona 1 — Events Manager (Karen)
Context
Karen manages 80–120 events per year ranging from free 30-minute Legal Helpline Q&As to multi-day annual conferences with hundreds of delegates. She currently builds events in Cvent and manually reconciles data in Dynamics 365. Her biggest pain points are double-entry, lack of real-time reporting, and the high cost per-registration that Cvent charges.
	•	Needs to create events quickly using templates, without requiring developer support.
	•	Must be able to manage waitlists, capacity, and cancellations in real time.
	•	Requires automated email workflows that trigger without manual intervention.
	•	Needs reporting dashboards she can share with senior leadership.
Persona 2 — REC Member (Delegate)
Context
A typical REC member registers for 3–5 events per year. They may be booking for themselves and/or colleagues. They expect a smooth, branded, mobile-responsive experience and want confirmation, joining instructions, and reminders without chasing.
	•	Must be able to find and register for events easily via the CS5 website.
	•	Expects instant confirmation email with calendar invite.
	•	May need to book multiple colleagues in a single transaction.
	•	Wants to manage their own bookings (transfer, cancel) without calling REC.
Persona 3 — Non-Member (Prospect)
Context
Non-members can attend some REC events at a higher price point. Registration creates a CRM contact record and triggers a membership follow-up workflow. This persona is a key acquisition channel for REC.
	•	Must be able to register and pay without holding a REC membership.
	•	Pays a higher ticket price; system must enforce differential pricing by registration type.
	•	Post-event, receives a targeted membership offer communication.
Persona 4 — Event Staff (Check-in)
Context
REC staff manage check-in at physical events using a mobile app. At larger events (Annual Conference), QR badge scanning is required. Staff need offline resilience in case of poor venue WiFi.
	•	Needs iOS and Android app with QR scanner for badge check-in.
	•	Must work in low-connectivity environments with offline sync capability.
	•	Needs to see live attendee dashboard and flag no-shows.

8. Glossary

Term
Definition
Azure
Microsoft cloud platform. Hosting environment for the REC Event Platform (App Service, PostgreSQL, Blob Storage, Key Vault, Application Insights).
Cvent
The incumbent event management SaaS platform being replaced by this custom-built solution.
CS5
REC's current Content Management System and website platform (rec.uk.com). The event calendar widget will be embedded here via iframe.
CPD
Continuing Professional Development. REC runs CPD-accredited training events for members. Post-event CPD certificates may be auto-generated.
D365 / Dynamics 365
Microsoft Dynamics 365 CRM — REC's system of record for member, contact, and commercial data.
DPA
Data Processing Agreement — a contractual requirement under UK GDPR between REC (data controller) and the vendor (data processor).
Entra ID
Microsoft's cloud identity platform (formerly Azure Active Directory). Used for SSO authentication of REC staff into the admin portal.
GPT-4o
OpenAI's multimodal large language model. Used in the Video Transcription App for summarisation and Q&A.
iframe
An HTML element that embeds content from one URL within a page at another URL. Used to surface the event calendar on the CS5 website without rebuilding the CS5 CMS.
London Systems
REC's managed IT service provider. Responsible for Entra ID configuration, Azure provisioning support, and Dynamics 365 infrastructure.
MoSCoW
Prioritisation method: MUST have, SHOULD have, COULD have, WON'T have (this phase). Used to classify functional requirements.
Mux
Video infrastructure platform used as the primary channel for video upload and audio extraction in the Video Transcription App.
OpenAI Whisper
OpenAI's speech-to-text transcription model. Used in the Video Transcription App for audio transcription.
PCI-DSS
Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard. SAQ-A is the simplified self-assessment questionnaire applicable when using a hosted payment page (Stripe).
PII
Personally Identifiable Information. Data that can identify an individual — subject to UK GDPR controls.
RBAC
Role-Based Access Control. A security model where access permissions are tied to defined roles rather than individual users.
React Native
Cross-platform mobile framework (Facebook/Meta) used to build the iOS and Android check-in app from a single codebase.
Stripe
Payment processing platform. Used as the exclusive card payment gateway. PCI-DSS compliant; no card data stored on REC systems.
TLS
Transport Layer Security. Encryption protocol for data in transit. TLS 1.2 minimum required.
